Object 01 — Robot Head  /  Hard Surface · Mechanical

SDF Method
Method 01 / 03
Signed Distance Fields
Uses smooth minimum (smin) to blend primitives. Panel seams, boolean cutouts, and eye lenses are exact. Surface is everywhere C¹ continuous — perfectly smooth, differentiable normals, zero seam artifacts.
  • Seamless smooth blending (smin kernel k=0.45)
  • Accurate gradient normals everywhere
  • Crisp boolean cutouts for grills & visors
  • Full analytic control over blend radius

Rule-Based
Method 02 / 03
Rule-Based (CSG Hard Union)
Identical box & sphere primitives combined with a hard boolean min(). A C⁰ discontinuity — a sharp ridge — forms wherever two surfaces intersect. No blending possible.
  • Visible sharp seams at every junction
  • Broken gradient → incorrect normals at creases
  • Shadow & AO banding at hard edges
  • No control over transition smoothness
INITIALIZING…
Procedural Noise
Method 03 / 03
Procedural Noise (fBm Displacement)
A sphere & box displaced by 3-octave sinusoidal noise. All crisp mechanical edges are lost — the head becomes blobby. Noise cannot represent distinct sub-objects cleanly.
  • No precise shape or topology control
  • Uniformly bumpy — no clean panel edges
  • Noisy normals cause specular artifacts
  • Cannot represent distinct mechanical parts
Object 02 — Tree  /  Recursive Branching · Organic Structure
INITIALIZING…
SDF Method
Method 01 / 03
Signed Distance Fields
Capsule SDFs blended with smin at every branch junction. The trunk-to-branch transition is perfectly smooth. Leaf clusters use sphere smin for organic softness with exact silhouettes.
  • Smooth branch-trunk merge with smin
  • Accurate normals for soft lighting
  • Leaf spheres blend naturally into canopy
  • Controllable blend radius per joint
INITIALIZING…
Rule-Based
Method 02 / 03
Rule-Based (L-System Fork)
Iterative stack-based L-system: each branch forks at a fixed 35° angle with 0.7× scale reduction. Perfectly deterministic and symmetric. Hard capsule unions create visible seam ridges at every fork.
  • Deterministic symmetric branching pattern
  • Hard seam ridge at every branch junction
  • Fixed angle rules — no organic variation
  • Lighting artifacts at capsule intersections
INITIALIZING…
Procedural Noise
Method 03 / 03
Procedural Noise (fBm Displacement)
Noise-twisted cylinder trunk and a sinusoidally displaced sphere crown. The silhouette is organic but the canopy has no distinct branches — just an undifferentiated bumpy blob.
  • Organic silhouette from noise displacement
  • No distinct branches — unified blob canopy
  • Cannot reproduce deterministic structure
  • Surface roughness uncontrollable globally

Method 02 / 03
Rule-Based (Diamond-Square)
Diamond-square quadrant rules: axis-aligned ridges, quadrant-signed hills, and diamond crosshatch pattern stacked deterministically. Terrain is angular and grid-aligned — no organic variation.
  • Purely deterministic, fully repeatable
  • Angular grid-aligned ridges and valleys
  • No organic irregularity or randomness

Method 03 / 03
Procedural Noise (fBm Heightfield)
6-octave fractal Brownian motion stacked over 3 frequencies. Produces the most geologically convincing terrain — but zero precision. No cliff at a specific location, no exact valley width.
  • Most organic & geologically convincing
  • Zero control over exact feature placement

  • Rich multi-scale detail from fbm octaves
